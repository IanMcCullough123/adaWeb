Summary

Founded in 1994 by John Borthwick and Benjamin Weil, äda 'web emerged as a unique platform that embraced the digital realm, encompassing online art, experimental literature, publishing, and exhibitions. Serving as a pseudo-internet startup, it facilitated partnerships among artists, web designers, and developers, fostering an environment for exploring the internet as a canvas for creativity and widespread distribution. This collaborative approach allowed äda 'web to transcend the confines of net art, presenting diverse works by artists specializing in digital media and those who viewed internet engagement as a seamless extension of their conceptual artistic pursuits.
