Summary

Founded in 1994 by John Borthwick and Benjamin Weil, äda 'web operated as a platform that embraced the digital realm, including online art, experimental literature, publishing, and exhibitions. As a pseudo-internet startup, it facilitated partnerships between artists, web designers, and developers, thus fostering an environment where the internet could be explored as a canvas for creativity and as a channel for widespread distribution.

This distinctive collaborative nature of the site empowered äda 'web to break free of confines associated with net art. It presented a variety of works by artists who specialized in digital media, as well as those who regarded engagement with the internet as a seamless extension of their conceptual artistic pursuits.
